The Two-Stage Pipeline

TurboQuant compresses the KV cache in two complementary stages: PolarQuant makes scalar quantization MSE-optimal via random rotation, then QJL corrects residual inner-product error with a 1-bit sketch.

Random Rotation Intuition

Real KV cache vectors are non-isotropic — energy concentrates along certain axes, making per-coordinate quantization suboptimal. A random orthogonal rotation spreads the energy uniformly, letting each coordinate be quantized independently with a known optimal grid.

Bit-Width vs Quality Tradeoff

Lower bits save memory but increase distortion. PolarQuant shifts the Pareto frontier down, and QJL correction pushes it even further — achieving near-zero distortion at just 3.5 bits.

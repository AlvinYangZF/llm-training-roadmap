Learning Rate Schedule

The learning rate isn't fixed — it follows a schedule. Warmup prevents early large updates from destroying weights. Cosine annealing smoothly reduces LR for fine-grained convergence.

Typical: warmup 2000 steps, peak LR = 3e-4, min LR = 3e-5

Warmup helps because large early gradients can destroy pre-trained weight structure. Starting with small updates lets the optimizer find a safe direction first.

Batch Size & Gradient Accumulation

You can't always fit large batches in GPU memory. Gradient accumulation simulates larger batches by summing gradients over multiple forward passes before updating weights.

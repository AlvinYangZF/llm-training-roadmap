Discovery Attention Sparsity

Attention matrices in LLMs are extremely sparse. Most query-key interactions produce near-zero attention weights, while a tiny fraction of tokens dominate the total attention mass.

Policy H2O Eviction Strategy

H2O uses a hybrid eviction policy that combines three categories of tokens to maintain generation quality while minimizing cache size.

Attention Sinks
Heavy Hitters
Recent Window
Evicted
  • Attention Sinks (first 4 tokens): The initial tokens consistently receive high attention across all layers and heads, regardless of content. Always retained.
  • Heavy Hitters (top-k by cumulative score): Tokens that accumulate the highest total attention weight across decoding steps. Dynamically identified and updated.
  • Recent Window (last W tokens): The most recently generated tokens provide essential local context. Always retained with a sliding window.
  • Evicted: All remaining tokens are removed from the KV cache. Their attention contribution is negligible.
Cache = Sinks(4) + HeavyHitters(k) + Recent(W) ≪≪ Full Cache(N)

Top-k and Top-p (Nucleus) Sampling

Top-k keeps only the k most probable tokens. Top-p (nucleus) keeps tokens until their cumulative probability reaches p. Both truncate the tail to avoid unlikely tokens.

Repetition Penalty & Special Techniques

Repetition penalty reduces the probability of tokens that have already appeared, preventing repetitive text. Adjust the penalty factor to see its effect.

1.0 (No penalty)

Presence Penalty

Flat penalty applied once per token that has appeared. Encourages new topics.

Frequency Penalty

Penalty proportional to how often a token appeared. Discourages word repetition.

Contrastive Decoding

Subtract a weaker model's distribution from the stronger model. Amplifies what the expert knows that the amateur doesn't.

Speculative Decoding

Small draft model proposes tokens; large model verifies in parallel. Same quality, 2-3x faster inference.

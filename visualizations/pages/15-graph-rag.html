Problem

Why Vector RAG Fails on Global Questions

Vector RAG retrieves chunks similar to the query. But for questions like "What are the main themes?", no single chunk contains the answer — it requires reasoning across the entire corpus.

Vector RAG

Q: "What are the main themes in this dataset?"
Chunk 47: "...meeting discussed budget cuts..."
Chunk 231: "...quarterly revenue increased..."
Chunk 89: "...new product launch planned..."
Random chunks — no global view. Answer is incomplete and unfocused.

GraphRAG

Q: "What are the main themes in this dataset?"
Community A: "Financial performance and revenue trends"
Community B: "Product strategy and market expansion"
Community C: "Organizational restructuring and cost optimization"
Community summaries capture global themes. Answer is comprehensive and structured.

Algorithm

Leiden Community Detection

The Leiden algorithm clusters graph nodes into communities of related entities. GraphRAG uses hierarchical levels — zoom out for broader themes, zoom in for details.

Pipeline

Map-Reduce Answer Generation

GraphRAG uses a map-reduce pipeline: each community generates a partial answer, then they're combined into a comprehensive global answer.

1
Query: "What are the main themes?"
2
MAP: Community A → "Financial trends: revenue up 15%, Q3 record..."
3
MAP: Community B → "Product strategy: 3 new launches, expansion to Asia..."
4
MAP: Community C → "Restructuring: 2 divisions merged, 20% cost reduction..."
5
REDUCE: Rank partial answers by helpfulness → Combine into global response
6
Final Answer: Three main themes emerged: (1) Strong financial performance, (2) Aggressive product expansion, (3) Organizational efficiency...

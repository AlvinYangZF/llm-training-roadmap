Phenomenon

Lost in the Middle

Liu et al. (2023) showed that LLMs recall information best at the start and end of the context, but accuracy drops sharply for information in the middle. This is why RAG can beat long context: it places relevant info at the beginning.

Architecture

The Hybrid Future

The emerging approach combines both: hot data in preloaded KV cache (CAG), cold data via vector search (RAG), and prompt caching (Anthropic/Google) as a commercial middle ground.

⚡

Hot Data (CAG)

Frequently accessed, small corpus. Preloaded into KV cache for instant retrieval.

🔗

Prompt Caching

Commercial middle ground (Anthropic, Google). Cache frequently used prefixes at reduced cost.

🔍

Cold Data (RAG)

Large corpus, rarely accessed. Vector search on demand for targeted retrieval.

CAG cost = context_tokens x price_per_token (flat) | RAG cost = embedding + search + retrieved_tokens (scales with KB)
